Memo: Use Cases for Running Squadron Scramble

Rachel Ober and Brandon Schory

Use Cases:

1. "Players register with administrator"

  1. Player provides username to request to join a game
  2. While there are still empty seats, administrator adds users to game
  3. If the minimum number of players to run a game have joined, the administrator waits a maximum of two minutes for other players to join, before starting the game
  4. When seats are full, or the two minutes expire, administrator closes registration

2. "Player takes turn"

  1. The player takes exactly one card from the face-down deck, or at least one card from the face up deck
  2. The player then checks if there are any squadrons in his hand, and puts them on the table
  3. For each squadron of Fighter cards the player places on the table, he may "shoot down" another player's squadron of bombers, such that:
    1. If it is an axis squadron of fighters, he may shoot down an allied squadron of bombers
    2. If it is an allied squadron of fighters, he may shoot down an axis squadron of bombers
  4. The player gains ownership of any shot down squadrons, and no one else can attack them (take ownership of them)
  5. If the player has placed the last card in his hand on the table, the battle and the player's turn ends
  6. If the player has not placed the last card in his hand on the table, but places his last card face up on the face-up deck, the battle and his turn ends
  7. Otherwise, the player places one card, face up on the face-up deck, and his turn ends

3. "Administrator plays a complete game"

  1. The Administrator registers some number of users for the game, per use case 1
  2. The Administrator shuffles the deck and gives each player 7 cards
  3. The Administrator randomly selects a player to begin the battle
  4. Each player takes turns in a clockwise order, per use case 2, starting from the randomly selected player
  5. When any player puts down the last card in his hand, the Administrator ends the battle
  6. The Administrator awards each player 15 points for each figther squadron they have on the table, and 30 points for each bomber squadron
  7. If no player's score is greater than or equal to 250, the Administrator starts a new battle by returning to step (2) above
  8. When any player's score is 250 or greater, the game ends and the player with the highest score wins
